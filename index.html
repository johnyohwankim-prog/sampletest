About

I am a senior at Westlake High School with a deep interest in physical and analytical chemistry — particularly how small changes in molecular structure drive macroscopic material properties. What draws me to chemistry is the discipline's demand for both precision and imagination: you design an experiment, collect imperfect data, and still have to tell a truthful story about what the molecules did.

Since sophomore year, I have worked in university and hospital-affiliated labs, contributed to two co-authored papers, and learned to treat a lab notebook as seriously as a manuscript draft. I am applying to college to study chemistry with the goal of pursuing graduate research in materials science and sustainable catalysis.
